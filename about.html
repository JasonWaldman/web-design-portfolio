About Me

I’m Jason Waldman, a communication and marketing professional currently working at the University of Denver. My background combines strategic communication, digital branding, and audience engagement. I focus on creating clear, user-centered experiences that make information easy to understand and act on.

Through my academic and professional work, I’ve developed skills in communication strategy, digital content development, and brand messaging. I’m especially interested in how structure, layout, and navigation influence the way users interact with digital platforms. My web design coursework has strengthened my understanding of HTML, accessibility standards, and usability-driven design.

This portfolio reflects both my growth in front-end development and my broader experience in communication strategy. I aim to build websites that are clean, accessible, and organized — where users can quickly find what they need without confusion.

Professional Focus

  • Communication strategy and digital branding
  • User-centered website structure and navigation
  • Accessible, semantic HTML development
  • Improving design decisions using usability feedback

More About My Approach

My professional experience has reinforced the importance of clarity in communication — whether through messaging, layout, or user interaction. I pay close attention to how audiences scan content, where they expect to find information, and how small design adjustments can improve comprehension. As I continue developing my technical skills in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, my goal is to bridge strategy and execution by building digital experiences that are both visually clean and functionally intuitive.
